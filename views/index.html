About US

Mambure Trust is a girl empowerment organisation in Zimbabwe founded and registered in 2016 as a Trust (MA0000550/2016). The organisation was established following the realisation that rural schools lack infrastructural and institutional support to improve the quality of education for learners in these communities. We noted the widening technological divide between rural and urban schools, and between men and women. Mambure Trust therefore seeks to promote the empowerment of girls and women in rural schools and surrounding communities through education, effective and strategic use of digital literacy and other life skills. The organisation was also established to shift attitudes that discourage young girls and women from pursuing specific subjects and professions. Our broader vision is to see more girls and women partcipating in male dominated fields, which will contribute to the achievement of gender equality. We advocate against stereotypical attitudes and beliefs that hinder girls from pursuing male dominated subjects and careers. Our programmes revolve around Education, Gender Equality and Equity, and Women Empowerment.

Our mission is to empower communities, strengthen civil society, and promote social change in partnership with local development agencies which act as community-driven responses to social justice.

ICT skills training programme

This programme promotes access to and usage of ICT –by female teachers and learners in rural schools. We provide basic computer literacy training – in rural schools which lack ICT infrastructure.

WHAT WE DO

Debate and Essay Competitions

We organise debate and essay competitions to build confidence and leadership skills among girls in rural schools. The competitions promote visibility of the voices of girls in rural areas to the wider community. In 2021, we successfully hosted a district essay writing competition for primary and secondary school girls in Bikita to commemorate the International Day of the Girl Child, where ten girls walked away with various prizes.

Career Guidance and Mentorship Programme

It is well documented that many girls give up on Maths and Science subjects at an early age because of the way these are usually framed as difficult and subject for boys. This is further worsened by a lack of female teachers who are teaching these subjects, leaving girls without role models to motivate them into taking these subjects .
